Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is a flavorful West African dish made by stir-frying cooked rice with vegetables, spices, and proteins like chicken or shrimp. The rice is first parboiled, then cooked in a lightly seasoned broth before being stir-fried with mixed vegetables and aromatics for a colorful, fragrant dish.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, sweet corn, green beans)
  • 1 cup diced cooked chicken or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents sticky rice.
  2. Parboil the rice: Add the rice to a pot with chicken stock, salt, curry, and thyme. Cook until the rice is firm (about 70% done). Do not let it get soft. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add mixed vegetables, bell peppers, and any protein (chicken or shrimp). Stir-fry for 3–5 minutes.
  5. Add the parboiled rice into the pan. Stir continuously to combine everything evenly.
  6. Season with curry, thyme, salt, and pepper. Continue stir-frying on medium-high heat for 5–10 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and fragrant.
  7. Remove from heat and serve hot.
