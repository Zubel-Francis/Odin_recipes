Jellof Rice

Description

To make Jollof rice, you first create a rich, blended pepper and tomato base, which is then fried with aromatics and spices. Washed long-grain parboiled rice is added to this base with stock, covered tightly, and steamed until fluffy.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large red onion, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, peeled and minced
  • 1 large red bell pepper, roughly chopped
  • 1 large tomato, roughly chopped
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (or habanero, adjust to taste for heat)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 stock cube (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (optional: peas, carrots, green beans)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch, which helps prevent mushy rice. Drain and set aside in a colander.
  2. Prepare the pepper mix: In a blender, combine the red bell pepper, tomato, Scotch bonnet, half of the onion, garlic, and ginger. Blend until completely smooth to create the pepper base.
  3. Saut√© the aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Slice the remaining half of the onion and fry for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  4. Cook the tomato paste and base: Stir in the tomato paste and fry with the onions for about 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until the color darkens and the oil starts to separate. Carefully pour in the blended pepper mix.
  5. Simmer the sauce: Add thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves. Cook the sauce, stirring occasionally, for 15-20 minutes until it reduces and thickens. The oil will rise to the top when it is ready.
  6. Add stock and rice: Heat the stock in a separate saucepan until hot. Add the hot stock, stock cube (if using), and salt to the reduced sauce and bring to a boil. Gently stir in the drained rice until it is fully coated in the sauce. Add frozen vegetables now, if using.
  7. Cook the Jollof rice: Reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Cover the pot tightly with a lid (use aluminum foil under the lid to seal in the steam if needed). Cook for 25-30 minutes without opening the lid.
  8. Rest and fluff: Turn off the heat and let the pot sit, covered, for 5-10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.
